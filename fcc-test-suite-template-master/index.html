Albert Einstein

The most influential scientists

  • Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist who is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. He is best known for his development of the theory of relativity, which revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and gravity.
  • Einstein was born in Ulm, Germany and grew up in Munich. He showed an early interest in mathematics and physics, and after finishing school, he moved to Switzerland to study at the Polytechnic School in Zurich. It was there that he began his groundbreaking work in theoretical physics.
  • In 1905, Einstein published a series of papers that had a profound impact on the field of physics. These included his theory of special relativity, which showed that the laws of physics are the same for all observers in uniform motion, and his famous equation E=mcÂ², which established the equivalence of mass and energy.
  • Einstein continued to work on the theory of relativity throughout his career, developing the more complex theory of general relativity in 1915. He also made important contributions to the field of quantum mechanics, and was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
  • In addition to his scientific work, Einstein was also an outspoken advocate for pacifism and civil rights. He was a member of the NAACP, and spoke out against segregation and discrimination in the United States.
  • Einstein died in 1955 at the age of 76, but his work continues to influence physicists and scientists today. His theories have been tested and verified through numerous experiments, and his ideas have shaped our modern understanding of the universe.
